Standard Statement — DM.10

The hospital uses integrated clinical, operational, and quality data analytics to drive measurable improvements in care quality, patient safety, and performance, supported by early-warning capabilities, population health contribution, research data governance, and a data-driven improvement culture.

Research Data Governance

Survey Process Preparation

📄 Document Review (DR)
  • Integrated analytics examples combining ≥2 data sources
  • Early-warning / predictive capability documentation & results
  • Population health registry contributions (NCHADS, CENAT, CNM)
  • Research data governance policies, ethics review, data sharing agreements
  • ≥2 documented data-driven improvement examples with sustained results
💬 Interview (I)
  • Department heads: "How does data inform your top improvement priorities?"
  • Clinical leaders: "Give an example of a change made because of quality data."
  • Staff at all levels: "What quality indicators are relevant to your work?"
  • Technical Office: "How are data analytics used to detect early risk signals?"
👁 Observation (O)
  • Quality indicator displays visible in clinical areas (run charts, boards)
  • Evidence of data discussed in team meetings (meeting structure)
  • Data management tools accessible and in active use
  • Staff can point to data displays and explain what they show
⚖️

Scoring Criteria

✓ MET

Integrated analytics generating actionable intelligence; early-warning capabilities appropriate to digital maturity; contributing to population health; research data governance robust; data-driven culture with ≥2 documented improvement examples.

◑ PARTIALLY MET

Some analytics but not integrated; OR early-warning limited; OR population health contribution minimal; OR research governance incomplete; OR fewer than 2 documented data-driven improvements with sustained results.

✗ NOT MET

No data analytics beyond basic reporting, OR no evidence of data-driven improvement across the organization at any level.
